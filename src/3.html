Coils, accessories and positioning
I can identify coils and the examinations for which they are used.
I can inspect coils for damage and describe the effects of poor coil handling.
I can connect coils in a manner that prevents damage to the coil, connectors, cable, and tabletop.
I can position coil cables and infusion lines to prevent injury to the patient during table movement.
I can demonstrate the correct positioning of flex coils.
I can demonstrate the incorrect positioning of flex coils.
I can find additional coil information in the Instructions for Use.
I can safely store coils.
I can use accessories to:
Prevent skin to bore contact
Prevent skin to skin contact
Make the patient comfortable.
Immobilize the patient to reduce movement during the examination.

Emergency Magnet Stop Button
I can locate the emergency magnet off button.
I can describe the events and potential risks that occur when the emergency shut down is initiated.
I recognize the asphyxiation and burn dangers associated with release of cryogens that occurs during emergency shut down.
I can identify emergencies when the emergency magnet off button is used and not used.
I recognize that the emergency magnet off button (quench) is used only when there is risk of imminent danger.
